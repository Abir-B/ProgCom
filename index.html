Empirically-driven Software Comprehension Research

Leveraging insights from common patterns in software comprehension strategies to help optimize programming tasks

The Project

stands for program comprehension, a cooperation project of the University of Hamburg and the University of Bremen operating under the direction of Prof. Walid Maalej and Prof. Rainer Koschke.

The goal of the project is to advance research focusing on how developers and humans in general understand and reason about code. The main areas of interest include investigating abstract mental representations of source code and strategies followed in order to search for crucial information contained therin. The project team is composed of reasearchers knowledgeable in the fields of computer science and psychology enabling a holistic view of the investigated research problem. The outcomes are highly relevant for optimizing tedious development tasks such as code maintenance and bug fixing, which are known to consume considerable amounts of resources.

ProgCom is funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG) and is an exension of the PUNGA project, which started in 2009 spanning three years of research.

Data-Driven

Extracting insights from various software engineering artifacts such as source code and IDE interactions among others

Analysing Code

Investigating code complexity metrics and their correlation with developers' perception

Focusing on developers

Conducting empirical studies to gain a founded understanding of common development practices and the tools used to assist with program comprehension
